Student guide

Plan your schedule with Class Planner

Class Planner uses course times, your preferences, and your personal commitments to build schedule options. You can compare them before you enroll.

Check every option. Course times may be missing or listed as TBA, and seats can change.

1 Build your plan

Find courses you want to take

Choose a term. Then search by course code or title. You can search with part of a course name or number, and you do not need to type leading zeroes.

  • Results show section counts, open seats, and waitlist spots when that information is known.
  • Add 1 to 12 courses.
  • Seat and waitlist counts can change. They do not save you a spot.

2 Set preferences

Tell the planner what works for you

Choose Open or waitlist or Open only. Then rank these goals: Fewer days, Later starts, and Shorter gaps.

  • Set class-time limits with Start no earlier and Finish by.
  • Add the days and times when you would prefer to take classes.
  • Add fixed times in your personal schedule as weekly or specific-date commitments.

Add your personal schedule and commitments

Add times when you are busy, such as work, practice, appointments, or other plans. Class Planner will not place classes during those times.

You can change these settings later from Options.

3 Compare and review

Compare your schedule options

Use Previous and Next to move through your proposed schedules. Best match based on your priorities is the option that best fits your settings. Other options may also work.

Read the full calendar

The calendar shows every scheduled class meeting. Exams are in a separate table below it. Check both before you choose.

Check the review status

No conflicts means no review items were reported.

4 Adjust sections

Keep the sections you like and try other options

Select a course block to see its details. Choose Lock Course to keep its current sections while you view other schedules. Unlock it when you want Class Planner to try different sections.

Try a different set of sections

Swap Section lets you compare other lecture, discussion, and lab groups before you change your schedule.

Current
The sections now on your schedule.
Fits current schedule
The sections do not create a time conflict.
Preview
Shows these sections on your calendar without saving them.
Use this section
Adds these sections to your schedule.

Seat counts can change. Previewing or choosing sections does not save you a seat.

Check schedule details

Open Details to see meeting and exam times, rooms, instructor, seats, waitlist, and section ID. It also shows when the information was last updated.

If information is missing or old, check it in TSS before you decide.

5 Save, share, and add to your calendar

Save or share your schedule

Save & share creates a short link. You can copy it and open it later. A shared schedule shows its review status. Choose Edit to return to Class Planner and make changes.

Add to Calendar lets you download a calendar file or open Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook. This only copies the class times. It does not enroll you or update when a class changes.

i

Remember: Class Planner does not enroll you

You still need to enter your course and section choices in TSS, the enrollment system, during your enrollment time. Class Planner does not save you a seat.

Frequently asked questions

Know before you plan

Does Class Planner enroll me in classes?

No. It only helps you plan. Enter your final course and section choices in the TSS enrollment system during your enrollment time.

Are seats or waitlist positions guaranteed?

No. Seat and waitlist counts can change. Class Planner does not save a seat or waitlist spot.

What should I do with a Review warning?

Open the review items and check the affected meetings or missing information. You may need to change the schedule or check the course in TSS. See Compare and review options.

What if a course has TBA or missing meeting information?

Class Planner cannot check a meeting time it does not have. Look up the course in TSS and leave room for the missing meeting.

Can I change a saved schedule?

Yes. Open the saved link and choose Edit. Class Planner will open with those sections so you can make changes.

Can I share a schedule?

Yes. Save & share creates a short link you can copy. Anyone with the link can view that saved schedule.

Can I add the schedule to my calendar?

Yes. Choose Add to Calendar, then pick a calendar app or download the calendar file. This copies the schedule once. It does not update when course information changes.

How many courses can I add?

You can add 1 to 12 courses. See Build your plan to get started.
